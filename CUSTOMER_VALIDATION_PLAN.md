## Phase 3: Conduct 10 Discovery Interviews (Week 2)

### **Interview Guide (20 minutes each)**

#### **Opening (1 min)**
```
"Thanks for taking time. I'm researching how RevOps teams work,
specifically where they spend time and what frustrates them.
This is just research - I'm not pitching anything yet."
```

#### **Problem Discovery (8 mins)**

**Ask:**
1. "Walk me through your week. What takes up most of your time?"
   
2. "Of those activities, which ones feel like a waste of time?
   Which ones are just... work?"
   
3. "If you could delegate ONE thing to AI/automation, 
   what would have the biggest impact?"
   
4. "Specifically, what do you spend time on that a machine 
   could theoretically do better?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ Data cleanup mentioned repeatedly → Your opportunity
- ✅ "I spend 10 hours/week on..." → Quantifiable pain
- ✅ "If only there was a tool that..." → Unprompted need
- ✅ "We pay Clari but it doesn't..." → Competitive gap

#### **Current Solutions (5 mins)**

**Ask:**
1. "What tools are you using right now?"
   
2. "Are you considering or using things like Clari, Gong, 
   Salesforce Einstein?"
   
3. "What's working? What's not?"
   
4. "What would your ideal solution look like?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ "We use Clari but it's too expensive"
- ✅ "We tried X but it didn't work"
- ✅ "We do this manually because tools don't..."
- ✅ "We'd switch if..."

#### **Willingness to Pay (4 mins)**

**Ask:**
1. "How much do you currently spend on RevOps tools per year?"
   
2. "If a tool saved you 10 hours/week, what would that be worth?"
   
3. "Would you pay $299/month for that? $499? $999?"
   
4. "When would you want to try something like this?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ "I'd pay $X for Y outcome"
- ✅ "That saves me $[value], so up to $[budget]"
- ✅ "We need to buy something this quarter/month"

#### **Closing (2 mins)**

**Ask:**
1. "If I built a tool that [your best guess at solution], 
   would you want to try it?"
   
2. "Can I follow up in 2 weeks with a prototype/demo?"
   
3. "Who else should I talk to?"
   
4. "What's the best way to reach you?"

## Phase 4: Analyze Your Findings (End of Week 2)

### **Success Metrics:**

#### **Problem Validation:**
```
✅ VALIDATED if 7+ of 10 people mention the SAME problem
❌ NOT VALIDATED if problems are scattered/different

Example:
- 8 people: "Data cleanup takes too much time"
- 7 people: "Forecast is always wrong"
- 6 people: "Reps don't update CRM"
= Data cleanup is your target problem
```

#### **Willingness to Pay:**
```
✅ VALIDATED if 5+ people say "I would pay $X"
✅ SUPER VALIDATED if they say "I'd pay that TODAY"
❌ NOT VALIDATED if budget unclear or too low

Example:
- 6 people: "I'd pay $299-499/month to save 10 hours"
- 4 people: "We'd buy this quarter"
- 3 people: "Our VP already approved budget for this"
= You have paying customers waiting
```

#### **Time to Pay:**
```
✅ FAST if: "We need this NOW/this month/this quarter"
⚠️ SLOW if: "We'd consider it next year"
❌ DEAD if: "We'll wait and see"

Only count people saying FAST or SUPER FAST.
```

## Phase 5: Lean Into What Resonates (Week 3)

### **If Data Quality is the clear winner:**

#### **Go Deeper:**
- Interview 5 MORE RevOps people about data quality specifically
- Ask: "What's the #1 data issue costing you?"
- Answers will be:
  - Missing close dates
  - Wrong deal amounts
  - Empty fields
  - Duplicate records
  - Stale contacts

#### **Find the High-Value Segment:**
- Ask each person: "What data issue causes the biggest problems?"
- Find the intersection:
  - Problem: Missing close dates
  - Impact: Can't forecast accurately
  - Cost: "That costs us $2M in forecast error per quarter"
  - Willingness to pay: "$500/month to fix it automatically"

#### **Quick Customer Interview Script:**

"Imagine a tool that:
- Scans your CRM every day
- Finds missing close dates
- Intelligently fills them in based on your patterns
- Shows you what it did (audit trail)
- Improves its accuracy over time

How much time would that save you?"

> Expected answer: "5-10 hours/week"

"What would that be worth?"

> Expected answer: "$300-500/month"

"Would you pay that TODAY if it existed?"

> If YES: You have a paying customer
> If NO: Ask why → iterate your solution

## Red Flags During Interviews:

```
🚨 RED FLAG: "It would be nice to have"
   → Not urgent enough. Move on.

🚨 RED FLAG: "We'd need to talk to the team first"
   → They're not the decision maker. Get intro to who is.

🚨 RED FLAG: "We can't spend more than $50/month"
   → Budget too low. Keep them for later, focus on bigger spenders.

🚨 RED FLAG: "We'll think about it"
   → Not interested. Don't waste time. Thank them and move on.

✅ GREEN FLAG: "I would pay for this TODAY"
   → This is a paying customer waiting.

✅ GREEN FLAG: "Our VP already has budget for this"
   → Even better - go talk to the VP.

✅ GREEN FLAG: "We need this by [date]"
   → Urgency = willingness to pay.
```

## Success Criteria (When to Build)

### **You should build if:**

```
✅ 7+ people mention the SAME core problem
✅ 5+ people say "I'd pay $X"
✅ 3+ people say "I'd pay TODAY" or "We need this this quarter"
✅ The problem costs them quantifiable money
✅ They can't easily solve it another way
✅ They're actively looking for a solution
```

### **You should PIVOT if:**

```
❌ Problems are too scattered (no pattern)
❌ Nobody wants to pay (value too low)
❌ All problems are "nice to have" (not urgent)
❌ They're already using Clari/similar (won't switch)
❌ They say "wait for Salesforce to build this"
```

### **You should PAUSE if:**

```
❓ Unclear problem statement
❓ Willingness to pay = zero
❓ Too early (they don't have budget yet)
❓ You need more data
```
